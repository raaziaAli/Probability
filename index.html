📘 Big idea Every chance event lives on the line from 0 to 1. The closer to 1, the more likely it is to happen. Exactly ½ means it's just as likely to happen as not — like a fair coin flip.

📘 Theoretical vs Experimental Theoretical probability is what the math predicts (e.g., red = ⅜ = 0.375). Experimental probability is what actually happened (e.g., 38 reds in 100 spins = 0.38). The more times you spin, the closer they get. This is called the Law of Large Numbers.
